The bases \(b > 9\) for which \(17_b\) divides \(97_b\) are found by interpreting the numbers in base \(b\).

In base \(b\), \(17_b = b + 7\) and \(97_b = 9b + 7\). The condition that \(b + 7\) divides \(9b + 7\) requires that \(b + 7\) is a positive divisor of 56 and greater than 16 (since \(b > 9\) implies \(b + 7 > 16\)).

The positive divisors of 56 are 1, 2, 4, 7, 8, 14, 28, and 56. The divisors greater than 16 are 28 and 56.

- If \(b + 7 = 28\), then \(b = 21\).
- If \(b + 7 = 56\), then \(b = 49\).

Both bases are greater than 9, and the digits 9 and 7 are valid in these bases (since the base is greater than the highest digit).

Verification:
- For \(b = 21\), \(17_{21} = 21 + 7 = 28\) and \(97_{21} = 9 \times 21 + 7 = 189 + 7 = 196\). Since \(196 / 28 = 7\), which is an integer, \(28\) divides \(196\).
- For \(b = 49\), \(17_{49} = 49 + 7 = 56\) and \(97_{49} = 9 \times 49 + 7 = 441 + 7 = 448\). Since \(448 / 56 = 8\), which is an integer, \(56\) divides \(448\).

No other bases satisfy the conditions, as other divisors of 56 are less than or equal to 16, and negative divisors or non-integer bases are not considered standard.

The sum of the bases is \(21 + 49 = 70\).
